A Glimpse into Our Rich Heritage and Vibrant Community

Welcome to Barangay Ili Sur

Ili Sur has a storied history that spans centuries, from its early indigenous roots through Spanish colonial influences to its present-day status as a thriving community

Pre-Colonial and Colonial Periods:

Like many areas in the Philippines, the region around San Juan, including Ili Sur, would have been influenced by local indigenous groups before Spanish colonization. Upon the arrival of the Spanish in the 16th century, the coastal areas of La Union, including San Juan, became pivotal for their role in maritime activities and as part of the galleon trade. The Spanish influence likely led to the establishment of organized barangay units, the introduction of Christianity, and the construction of churches and other infrastructures.

American and Japanese Occupation

During the American occupation in the early 20th century, the introduction of a new educational system, improvements in infrastructure, and changes in governance would have affected barangays like Ili Sur. World War II brought significant hardship to the Philippines, and areas like La Union would have experienced occupation by Japanese forces, which impacted local governance and daily life.

Post-Independence Era

After the Philippines gained independence in 1946, regions like La Union began developing more local economies, focusing on agriculture, fishing, and later on, tourism. San Juan, including Ili Sur, gradually became known for its beautiful beaches, attracting tourists and boosting local businesses.
